Welcome to Georgia Tech's REUL Lab Iconographic Tool

What is an EULA?

End-user licensing is the effort by which producers of software, websites, mobile applications, and consumer products with embedded software (including the so-called Internet of things) attempt to define their legal relationships with the consumers or users of their products. The producers are licensors, and the consumers or end-users are the licensees. The texts that embody these legal terms can broadly be considered license agreements, but they go under many different names: end-user license agreements or EULAs, terms of use or TOUs, terms of service or TOS, terms of agreement, software license agreements, and others.

What does our tool do?

This tool scans a EULA and presents users with iconographic representations of certain categories of information typically present in a EULA. These icons provide users with a visual summary of the document, which allows them to quickly scan and understand what the agreement contains.
